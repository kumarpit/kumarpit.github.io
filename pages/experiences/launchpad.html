Launchpad is a student-run software engineering club on campus that aims to simulate an industry development environment. I learned a tremendous amount at Launchpad - it was my first exposure to an AGILE based team, I attended my first scrum meeting, closed my first ticket, and learned about the various roles a software engineer could play in a project, i.e frontend, backend and devops.


What we did

In a team of 6 developers and 2 designers, we developed a mobile application called 'Ledge-it' to make daily finance management easier for university students. Our app included visualizations of monthly spending and incomes through pie charts and graphs, categorization, an easy-to-use UI, and the ability to simply scan a reciept to update spending logs.


What I did - Backend

We used the FastAPI framework to develop our server, and it was my first time using this framework - it turned out to be very intuitive. I setup data models and created endpoints to perform CRUD operations on our MongoDB database. I setup a GCP S3 bucket and created endpoints to upload base64 encoded images to the S3 instance as well delete existing images. This turned out to be more difficult than I initially thought and required me to read the GCP documentation a lot more carefully before it finally worked.


I also worked on the authentication.
