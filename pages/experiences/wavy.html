Wavy aims to build an UBC-exclusive app to make it easier for students to find events on campus and groups of people to go with and meet. It's primary motive is to increase the reach of several events that many students may not hear of and make it easier to connect people with the same interests.

Overview

The Wavy team consisted of only three people (including me) and the entire app was being developed by a single developer before I joined (the other member handled the business side of things). While the backend was nearing completion, the frontend had a lot of work to be done. We hadn't finalized a UI, only the authentication screens had been implemented by then and overall, there wasn't much clarity on how to approach the frontend. My first few weeks were spent providing my perspectives on the app, as well the structure of the team. Fast forward a few months, we are now an organized team of 8 members, consisting of UI designers and frontend/backend/devops engineers.

Through this transition, I had my first experience as an interviewer, and now find myself leading the frontend team. This is definitely an initmidating prospect and I would be lying if I said I feel completely confident to play this role.


What I have learned so far - Technical

Our mobile client is built using React Native with TypeScript. I had been introduced to React Native at Launchpad, and using TypeScript seemed quite intuitive, so I didn't have much trouble getting started. However, I soon learned that even though I knew how to write react native code that worked, I didn't necessarily know how to write 'good' code. Discussions with the developement lead introduced me to design patterns I could follow in react native. Namely, I learned about the container design pattern, the differences between 'smart' and 'dumb' components, and also learned about and implemented higher-order components. I am currently reading more about Redux and connecting our frontend to our backend.
